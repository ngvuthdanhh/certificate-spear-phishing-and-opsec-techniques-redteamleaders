# Behavioral Models in Spear Phishing

This document covers psychological and behavioral models commonly leveraged in phishing analysis and defensive education.

---

## 1. Cialdini’s Influence Principles
Understanding why people comply with phishing attempts:

- **Authority** — request appears to come from a superior.
- **Urgency** — pushes fast decision-making.
- **Scarcity** — limited-time action triggers.
- **Liking** — familiarity or friendliness.
- **Consistency** — aligning with previous behaviors.
- **Social Proof** — appearing widely accepted.

---

## 2. Business Workflow Levers
These processes are commonly exploited:

- Invoice approval flows  
- HR notifications  
- Payroll updates  
- Vendor communications  
- Internal ticketing systems  

---

## 3. Emotional Activation Patterns
Phishing often relies on emotional triggers:

- Anxiety  
- Curiosity  
- Fear of missing out  
- Avoiding penalties  
- Organizational obligation
