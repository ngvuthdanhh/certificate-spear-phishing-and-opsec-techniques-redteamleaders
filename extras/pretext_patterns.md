# Pretext Patterns for Spear Phishing

This document explains common pretext structures for training, awareness, and defensive pattern recognition.

---

## 1. Organizational Communication Pretexts
Common narratives include:

- Policy update reminders  
- IT or helpdesk notices  
- Payment verification requests  
- Vendor status inquiries  
- Internal workflow notifications  

---

## 2. Narrative Anchors
Effective pretexts rely on:

- A believable origin  
- Realistic organizational pain points  
- Public or known corporate context  

---

## 3. Tone & Culture Matching
Pretexts must mirror internal:

- writing tone  
- communication style  
- terminology  
- email formatting  

---

## 4. Blue Team Red Flags
Indicators of phishing attempts:

- Off-cycle requests  
- Tone mismatch  
- Wrong communication timing  
- Sender inconsistencies
